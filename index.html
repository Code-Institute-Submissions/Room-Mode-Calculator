Room Modes Explained

Room modes are the frequencies that a room natually resonants at. Where the wavelength of frequency is equal to one of the room dimensions a standing wave occurs.

At the node points (zero points of the wave) the audibility of that frequency is significantly reduced. Conversely at the antinode points (points of maximim amplitude. The frequency appears louder. Avoid these spots in the room as listening positions or for placing microphones.
